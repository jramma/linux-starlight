Welcome to **Class 1** of our 32-hour (8 classes) preparation course for the **Linux Foundation Certified Sysadmin (LFCS)** certification!

## Class 1 Focus

The goal of this module is to break down theoretical barriers and bring you down to the raw terminal level. We will start by exploring the origins and operational paradigms of Linux, move through the technical steps to instantiate `headless` machines of pure power in the **Debian** family, and finally connect via secure protocols to complete our deployment.

### What Will You Master?

To be able to configure networks and web servers in the future, we must first build the following foundations:

1. **Philosophy and Ecosystem**: Files vs Devices, GNU Core Utilities, Kernel.
2. **Hardware Components and Storage**: UEFI systems, Disks (SATA, NVMe), Partitions (GPT), Filesystems (ext4) and the Boot Process (GRUB2 and Initramfs).
3. **Text-Mode Installation**: Pure system deployment, and local network connection in console (`nmcli`).
4. **Post-Deployment Configuration**: Primary superuser management (`sudo`), hostnames, and SSH secure channel deployment.
